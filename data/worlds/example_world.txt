city square
Pigeons crowd the cracked pavement. A lamppost leans at a permanent angle as if listening. Somewhere nearby a radio plays the same four bars on repeat. The fountain in the centre has been dry for years. Enter the market hall. Slip into the narrow alley.

market hall
Stalls crowd together under a vaulted iron ceiling. The smell of old cheese and motor oil mingles freely. A trader in a yellow coat counts coins without looking up. Half the stalls are empty, their awnings folded and tied. Back out to the square. Climb the maintenance stairs to the rooftop.

narrow alley
Walls close in on both sides, close enough to touch at once. A locked door on the left has three different locks, all different colours. Someone has chalked a map on the brickwork but the key is missing. Back out to the square. Emerge onto the canal bridge.

transit station
Fluorescent light hums at a frequency just beyond discomfort. The departure board shows destinations that no longer exist. A vending machine in the corner sells items with no labels. The platform is empty but the rails still vibrate. Climb back up to the square. Push through to the archive room.

rooftop
The city spreads in all directions, indifferent to being observed. A folding chair sits near the edge, facing nothing in particular. An old broadcast antenna blinks a slow red pulse. Someone has left a mug of something here, it is still warm. Descend back to the market hall. Cross the gap to the clocktower.

canal bridge
The canal below is the colour of old mirrors. A barge is permanently moored here, its windows covered with newspaper from years ago. The bridge railing has been repaired many times in many different styles. Back through the alley. Cross into the square.

archive room
Shelves of files reach the ceiling. Most are labelled in a system no one has explained. A single desk lamp illuminates a form that has been partially filled in for a very long time. The pencil is still sharp. Return to the transit station.

clocktower
The clock face is lit from behind by something other than electricity. The mechanism ticks at irregular intervals, as if counting something other than time. A small door near the top has no handle on the inside. It is always exactly this cold. Cross back to the rooftop. Descend all the way to the square.
